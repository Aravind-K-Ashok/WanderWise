Discover the Beauty of WAYANAD

Wayanad, nestled in the heart of Kerala’s Western Ghats, is a mesmerizing district filled with lush forests, misty hills, and sparkling waterfalls. A haven for nature lovers, it’s home to exotic wildlife, sprawling spice plantations, and serene lakes. With its cool climate and scenic trails, Wayanad offers the perfect escape from urban hustle, attracting adventure seekers, trekkers, and those looking to reconnect with nature.

Rich in tribal heritage and cultural significance, Wayanad presents a unique blend of natural and human history. From exploring ancient caves with Stone Age carvings to indulging in delicious Malabar cuisine, every moment in Wayanad is a step into a vibrant and tranquil world.

Places To Visit

Surrounded by evergreen forests, Pookode Lake is a scenic freshwater lake ideal for a calm paddle boat ride. The lake’s ambiance makes it a great picnic spot for families and nature enthusiasts alike, with charming walkways, a children’s park, and an aquarium nearby.

The Edakkal Caves are an archaeological treasure, featuring rock engravings from the Neolithic era. Accessible via a trek up Ambukuthi Hills, the caves tell stories of early civilizations through carvings of animals, human figures, and symbols.

As the largest earth dam in India, Banasura Sagar Dam is not only an engineering marvel but also a hub for recreation. Visitors enjoy speed boating on its vast reservoir and exploring the lush hills that frame its backdrop.

A part of the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve, this sanctuary is teeming with biodiversity. Jungle safaris reveal herds of elephants, bison, leopards, and an array of bird species in their natural habitat, making it a must-visit for wildlife lovers.

What To Do

Conquer the heights of Chembra Peak, Wayanad’s highest summit, through a thrilling trek. The journey rewards you with panoramic views and a glimpse of the heart-shaped lake nestled mid-way, symbolizing love and serenity.

Unwind with a peaceful boat ride across Pookode Lake, soaking in the cool breeze and scenic surroundings. It’s a favorite activity among tourists of all ages and a perfect way to end a day of exploration.

Spend a night under the stars amidst the wild — Wayanad offers exceptional camping experiences with bonfires, guided night safaris, and immersive nature walks for thrill-seekers and families alike.

Witness the roots of Kerala’s spice heritage with a plantation tour. Walk through aromatic fields of pepper, cardamom, and vanilla while learning traditional farming techniques from local growers.

How To Reach

Wayanad is easily accessible by well-maintained roads. It lies around 70 km from Kozhikode and 130 km from Mysore. Buses, taxis, and private vehicles regularly travel these scenic, winding mountain roads.

The nearest airport is Calicut International Airport (CCJ), located about 80 km away. From the airport, taxis and pre-arranged shuttles offer direct access to Wayanad’s key destinations.

Kozhikode Railway Station is the closest railhead with frequent trains from major Indian cities. From the station, Wayanad is a 2-hour scenic drive.

Recommendations

Treat your taste buds to the authentic flavors of Wayanad. Savor dishes like bamboo rice biryani, appam with curry, and local fish fry cooked in traditional spices.

Explore the local markets filled with handmade crafts, tribal artifacts, and organic spices. The vibrant evening bazaars in Kalpetta are ideal for buying souvenirs.

Take a break at cozy coffee shops hidden among the hills. Sip freshly brewed Wayanad coffee while enjoying stunning plantation views and reading a good book.

Plan your visit during local festivals like Vishu, Onam, or tribal harvest celebrations to witness the cultural vibrance of Wayanad through dance, music, and rituals.
