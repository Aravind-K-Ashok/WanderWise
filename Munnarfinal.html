MUNNAR

Have a Quick Look into Munnar

1. Eravikulam National Park

Eravikulam National Park is a breathtaking sanctuary nestled in the Western Ghats of Kerala, India. Spanning 97 square kilometers, it is renowned for its rolling grasslands, Shola forests, and diverse wildlife. The park is home to the largest population of Nilgiri Tahr, an endangered mountain goat species

2. Top Station

Top Station is a breathtaking viewpoint situated at 1,700 meters above sea level, offering panoramic views of the Western Ghats and the lush valleys below. It is famous for the Neelakurinji flowers, which bloom once every 12 years, covering the hills in a mesmerizing shade of blue. Historically, it was the highest railway station in the valley,used for transporting tea from Munnar to Tamil Nadu.

3. Anamudi Peak

Anamudi Peak, standing at 2,695 meters, is the highest peak in South India, often referred to as the "Everest of South India". Located within Eravikulam National Park, it is home to diverse wildlife, including Asian elephants, Nilgiri Tahrs, and Gaurs. The peak is a popular trekking destination, offering stunning views of dense forests, tea plantations, and flowing rivers.

4. Tea Museum

The KDHP Tea Museum, also known as the Kannan Devan Hills Plantation Tea Museum, offers a fascinating glimpse into Munnar’s rich tea heritage. Established by Tata Tea in 2005, the museum showcases the history, evolution, and processing of tea in the region. Visitors can explore antique tea-processing machinery, historical photographs, and live tea-processing demonstrations. A tea-tasting session allows guests to sample different varieties of tea, making it a must-visit for tea lovers.

5. Attukal Waterfalls

Attukal Waterfalls is a breathtaking cascade nestled between Pallivasal and Munnar, surrounded by lush greenery. The waterfall is particularly stunning during the monsoon season, when the water gushes down the rocks with immense force. The area is popular for trekking and hiking, offering scenic trails through the hills. Visitors can enjoy the misty atmosphere, fresh aroma of medicinal plants, and picturesque views, making it an ideal spot for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

What to Do in Munnar

  • Trek to Anamudi Peak – Enjoy the breathtaking views and natural surroundings.
  • Visit Tea Estates – Stroll through endless green tea gardens and visit the Tea Museum.
  • Wildlife Watching – Explore Eravikulam National Park and spot the Nilgiri Tahr.
  • Sunrise at Top Station – Wake up early for a magical view of clouds and hills.
  • Try Local Food – Savor Munnar’s special Kerala dishes and hot teas.
  • Relax by Waterfalls – Spend peaceful moments at Attukal or Lakkam Waterfalls.

How to Reach Munnar

  • By Air: The nearest airport is Cochin International Airport, about 110 km from Munnar.
  • By Train: Aluva is the nearest railway station (around 120 km).
  • By Road: You can drive or take a bus from Kochi, Madurai, or Thekkady. The roads are scenic but curvy — enjoy the views!
